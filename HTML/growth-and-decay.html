Growth and Decay

Mathematicians and scientists use the term exponential growth to refer to any quantity that increases

by a set proportion over a given period of time. The proportion increase stays the same as the value of

the quantity increases, so the quantity increases more quickly as it gets larger. Exponential growth is a

specific way that a quantity may increase over time. It occurs when the rate of change of a quantity with

respect to time is proportional to the quantity itself.

Formula:P(t)=A(1+r)^t

Where:

P(t)=ending value

A=Initial amount

r=rate

t=Time

When can you use exponential growth formula:

We could use this formula if you have a rate for a given amount of time (such as annually, daily, monthly, etc.) Remember that it grows exponentially!

If the constant of proportionality is negative, then the quantity decreases over time, and is said to be undergoing exponential decay instead. Or in simple words, exponential decay is where a certain amount of population decreases.

Formula: P(t)=A(1-r)^t

Where:

P(t)=ending value

A=initial amount

r=rate

t=Time

When can you use exponential decay:

We could use this formula if you have a rate for a given amount of time just like the exponential growth

But only use it when the percentage of the original amount is decreasing.
